The Foundation

What is the Constitution?

The Constitution of India is the supreme set of rules that governs how our country works. Think of it as the rulebook for the entire nation — it defines who holds power, what rights every citizen has, and how the government must behave.

📜

Adopted

26 November 1949 — the day our Constituent Assembly adopted the Constitution. Celebrated as Constitution Day.

🇮🇳

Enacted

26 January 1950 — Republic Day. India became a republic governed by its own Constitution.

⚖️

Longest

With 448 articles and 12 schedules, India has the world's longest written constitution for a sovereign nation.

Our Journey

Road to Republic

1857

First War of Independence

The Sepoy Mutiny — India's first major uprising against British rule — sparked a national consciousness that would eventually lead to full independence.

1885

Indian National Congress

Founded in 1885, the INC became the primary vehicle of the Indian independence movement, uniting leaders from across the subcontinent.

1915–1947

Gandhi's Era

Mahatma Gandhi transformed the independence movement with non-violent civil disobedience — Salt March, Non-Cooperation, Quit India Movement.

1946

Constituent Assembly

The Constituent Assembly convened to draft the Constitution. Dr. B.R. Ambedkar chaired the Drafting Committee and shaped India's foundational document.

15 Aug 1947

Independence!

India achieved independence from British rule. Jawaharlal Nehru delivered his iconic "Tryst with Destiny" speech at midnight.

26 Nov 1949

Constitution Adopted

After 2 years, 11 months, and 18 days of deliberations, the Constituent Assembly adopted the Constitution. Now observed as Constitution Day (Samvidhan Diwas).

26 Jan 1950

Republic Day

India became a fully sovereign republic. The Constitution came into force, replacing the Government of India Act 1935. Dr. Rajendra Prasad became the first President.
